# Running log notes

Week 12 of base building. The easy runs are finally easy — heart rate stays
under 145 at a pace that used to push 160. Aerobic base is boring to build
and impossible to fake.

Long run fueling experiment: a gel at 45 minutes instead of "when I feel
bad" (too late by definition). The last three long runs ended strong instead
of in a shuffle. Fueling early is cheap insurance.

The calf niggle came back after the track session. Pattern over the year:
speed work twice a week breaks me, once a week builds me. Writing it down
because I apparently need to re-learn it every training block.

Shoes: rotating two pairs seems to matter less than replacing them on time.
The log says both niggles this year started within two weeks of a pair
crossing 700km. New rule — retire at 600, no sentiment.

Sleep is the strongest predictor in the whole spreadsheet. Every run rated
"terrible" followed a night under six hours. Training plan adherence matters
less than going to bed.

Race pacing note for spring: the negative-split halves keep being the good
ones. Even effort, not even pace — uphill kilometers are allowed to be slow.
The watch's pace alert does more harm than good on rolling courses; set it
to heart rate zones instead.

Strength work moved to twenty minutes after easy runs instead of a separate
gym day. Adherence went from 40% to 90%. The best routine is the one that
attaches to an existing habit.

Base building conclusion for the season: mileage grew 25% with zero injury
days once speed work dropped to once a week and shoes retired on schedule.
The plan for next block writes itself.
