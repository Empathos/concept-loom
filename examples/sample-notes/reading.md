# Reading notes

Finished the systems-thinking book. The idea that sticks: stocks change
slowly even when flows change fast. Most of my impatience with habits,
savings, and fitness is expecting stock-like things to respond at flow
speed.

Note-taking method check-in: highlights I never revisit are write-only
memory. The weekly review where I turn highlights into two-sentence
summaries is where the actual retention happens. Capture is not the
bottleneck; revisiting is.

The biography's best lesson was negative space — what the subject said no
to. The calendar of refusals mattered more than the list of achievements.
Saying no is the compounding habit.

Re-read of the writing craft book: cut the throat-clearing paragraph. My
first paragraphs are consistently warm-up; the piece starts at paragraph
two. Delete-first-paragraph is now a standing edit rule.

Pattern across three productivity books this year: they all reduce to
"decide once, then automate the decision." Recurring calendar events,
default meals, capsule wardrobe, standing orders. Willpower is a flow;
systems are stocks.

The novel translated from Japanese was the best thing I read this quarter.
Note to self: my hit rate with translated fiction is far above my average.
Follow the signal — pick translated works deliberately, not accidentally.

Library over buying, round two: of the twelve books bought last year, I
re-opened three. The library queue also acts as a natural want-vs-need
filter — half the holds I cancel before they arrive. Decide once: default
to the library, buy only after a second read.

Reading before bed displaced scrolling three nights out of seven this
month. The physical book on the pillow is the trigger that works; the
e-reader hides behind the same lock screen as everything else.
